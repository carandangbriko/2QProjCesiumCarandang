Supporting a Nonbinary Friend

So what if you find out that someone, be it a friend, a family member, or anyone you know, comes out as nonbinary? Here are a couple of tips, just coming from me, to help you give some support to a nonbinary friend you know!

1. Recognize and respect their identity

Whether it be their gender identity itself, or the pronouns they use, it's very important to recognize and respect their identity. Even if it takes a while to get used to, you should at least make an attempt to try. Gender dysphoria, or the dissatisfaction of a particular part of gender experience, is a very common thing that happens to nonbinary people, and at the very least, you can try to minimize it happening.

2. Encourage and validate them

More often than not, a nonbinary person could feel a bit afraid to be proud of who they are. There are a lot of factors surrounding this, like pre-existing beliefs, the community someone's in, and a lot more. It helps a lot to encourage someone's gender identity and help them feel valid in society because of things like these.

3. Keep an open mind

Keeping an open mind is important, especially with dealing with concepts like these. If anything in this site is something that strikes you as new information, I implore you to do your own research on the topic. These concepts have been aroound for a while, though it's only now that there's light being shed on a lot of them.

This site aims to help more with shedding light on this topic, and I sincerely hope it has!
